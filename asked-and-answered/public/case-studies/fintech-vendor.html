Fintech vendor security review

A fintech startup is being onboarded as a vendor by a bank. The bank sends a 60-row security assessment with strict requirements: every answer must carry a permalink or document citation, and the bank may spot-check evidence visibility.

The risk

A wrong or uncited answer can trigger a 2–4 week security review delay or kill the deal outright. The worst failure mode is not a missing answer; it is a confident-sounding answer that cannot be evidenced.

What happened

Asked & Answered processed the 60-row assessment using the bank reviewer’s user OAuth token, so private engineering Slack evidence was never surfaced.

  • 40 rows were auto-answered with workspace citations.
  • 20 rows were routed to a human SME.
  • A row about cyber-liability insurance had no matching workspace evidence. The agent returned Needs SME instead of inventing a policy number.
  • The final export included the XLSX with citations and a Canvas audit artifact showing every approval.

Why the refusal is the win

In a compliance context, a wrong answer is worse than no answer. Asked & Answered’s fail-closed design treats missing evidence as a routing signal, not a prompt for the LLM to hallucinate.

“The worst-case scenario is telling a bank something we can’t prove. A&A refused to answer where we had no evidence, which is exactly what I wanted it to do.”
— Compliance Lead (illustrative)
