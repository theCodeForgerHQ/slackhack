How it works

From Slack promise to signed verdict.

Two of the five steps are human gates. The agent gathers and proposes; people confirm and sign.

01Agent
Capture

Kept detects a commitment in a Slack channel and stores it as a derived fact.

No raw message body stored.
02Human gate
Confirm

A human confirms the commitment is real before Kept tracks it.

Gate 1 prevents false positives.
03Agent
Gather

Kept collects evidence from the tools that actually ship the work.

Tickets, PRs, CI, deploys, flags.
04Agent
Compare

The evidence packet is evaluated against the promise.

LLM proposes; code decides.
05Human gate
Sign

A human signs the verdict and Kept posts closure back to Slack.

Gate 2 prevents false closure.

Integrations

Evidence from where the work actually ships.

Connect the delivery tools your team already uses. Kept reads them to assemble proof for every commitment — the promise starts in Slack, the evidence comes from everywhere the work actually happens.

Slack
Live promise source
GitHub Actions
Proof · CI conclusion
Jira
Proof · issue status
LaunchDarkly
Proof · prod flag state

Live in this build: Slack, GitHub Actions and Jira over REST, LaunchDarkly over MCP. Any source you haven't connected falls back to a simulated MCP connector for the offline demo — read-only, never writes back, stores only derived facts.

Not a ticket tracker

Kept doesn't replace Jira, Linear, or GitHub. It checks whether the promise made to the customer matches the evidence across those tools.

Security & privacy

Built for shared customer channels — without copying your workspace.

Kept verifies commitments while minimizing what it stores. It persists derived facts, not raw Slack message bodies, and keeps humans in control of confirmation and closure.

Derived facts only

Kept stores structured commitments, owners, due dates, and verdict metadata — not full Slack message bodies.

Two human gates

Humans confirm the promise and sign the verdict. Nothing closes automatically.

Tenant isolated

Workspace data and credentials are separated by tenant, end to end.

Evidence-based

Evidence checks are deterministic; humans approve the final verdict.

Thread-native

Closure is posted back where the promise happened, so context is preserved.

What Kept will and won't do

No automatic closure. No silent writes. No fake verification.

Kept is deliberate about what it will and won't do. Evidence goes in, a person signs the verdict, and closure is posted back where the promise was made — nothing is closed on autopilot.

What Kept does
Gathers evidence from your delivery tools, read-only
Requires a human to confirm every commitment
Requires a human to sign every verdict
Shows customers only signed verdicts
Stores derived facts, not message bodies
Posts closure back in the original thread
What Kept never does
Close a commitment automatically
Write to or change your delivery tools
Copy raw Slack message bodies
Leak internal sources to a customer view
Mark anything kept without a human signature

The agent assembles and proposes; deterministic code decides and a person signs. That boundary is the whole point — it's why "kept" means something to your customer.
