The problem

A closed ticket is not proof.

Customer promises are made in Slack, but the evidence is scattered across tickets, pull requests, deploys, flags, and status pages. The ticket for Acme's SSO fix said Done on Thursday — and every upstream signal agreed. Kept gathered them anyway, and kept the promise open until the one that mattered was checked.

Why it matters

What Kept prevents.

✗01 · False closure

A ticket says Done, but the flag is still off.

Upstream systems agree while the customer still can't reach the feature. Kept catches the gap before you post “done.”

02 · Promise drift

“Friday” becomes “soon” becomes silence.

Commitments soften a word at a time until they vanish. Kept quantifies the slide and flags it before the customer feels it.

◔03 · Evidence gaps

Merged and green ≠ customer-visible.

A PR and a passing build are necessary, not sufficient. Kept reconciles every signal that stands between code and customer.

○04 · Unowned commitments

Promises made in passing just vanish.

A sentence in a channel has no owner and no memory — unless something turns it into a tracked obligation. Kept does.

03

How Kept works

From a Slack sentence to a signed verdict.

Two human gates, where judgment matters. Everything between them is the agent — and a deterministic check, not the model, decides the verdict.

I

Heard

Kept detects a customer commitment in the channel the moment it's said.

II

Confirmed Gate 1 · confirm the promise

A human confirms the promise is real before Kept starts tracking it.

III

Gathered

The agent collects evidence from delivery systems via MCP — a tool bridge to GitHub, CI, deploys, flags & status: ticket, PR, CI, deploy, flag, status page.

IV

Weighed

The evidence packet is assembled and a verdict is computed. The LLM proposes; code decides.

V

Signed Gate 2 · sign the verdict

A human signs the verdict and Kept posts closure back to the original thread.
